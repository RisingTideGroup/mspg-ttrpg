Your Role

You are the closest advisor this crew has. The MC runs the room. The Table DM Brief tells you what to do. This document tells you what to know and what to share. Think of yourself as a pseudo-sponsor — guide them toward informed choices, not optimised builds.

Phase 1 — Shopping
Tell them the course shape early

Before they commit any gold, make sure they understand the three-segment structure. You don't need to give numbers. Say this:

"The race has three segments. The Open City favours Velocity — that's your opening stretch. The Canyon District is almost all Handling tiles. The Industrial Gauntlet is Resilience-heavy, with one brutal tile near the end. Your 100g decides which segments you shine in and which you'll feel."

Encourage them to sketch a route plan on their card before spending. That's what it's for.

When a table wants to specialise

Don't steer them away from it — specialising is valid and creates strong table identity. Just make sure they know the cost. Ask:

"You'll be strong through your home segment. What's your plan for the other two?"

That's enough. If they've thought about it, they'll have an answer. If not, they will now.

Hard off-attribute tiles are the real pinch point

A Hard tile with −2 attribute is roughly 30% chance of advancing. Players should know this — especially these clusters:

  • Segment 1 has three consecutive Hard tiles at 1-3, 1-4, and 1-5 — two are off-type for Velocity specialists.
  • Segment 2 has four Hard tiles, mostly Handling. A −2 Handling crew will grind through most of Segment 2.
The Aether Bridge — surface if Resilience is being skipped

Tile 3-3 is the only Brutal tile on the course. It's a Resilience tile. Fully invested Resilience crew: ~40% chance of advancing. A −2 Resilience crew: ~20%. Surface this late in Phase 1 if they're considering skipping Resilience entirely — not as a warning, just information:

"One tile near the end of the race is significantly harder than anything else on the course. It's a Resilience tile. Worth knowing before you finalise."
Maestro Anyx — flag early, don't over-explain

Anyx is Vendor 12 in Tinkerers' Lane. Deep integration, 40g, +1 to all three attributes plus Natural 1 breakdown immunity. Qualification: all three attributes must be at −1 or better — no exceptions, no gold upfront. The MC will call this out at Phase 1 opening. Your job: make sure your table understands that Anyx only works for crews that have spread their investment. A pure specialist won't qualify. Don't explain unprompted — let them discover it. If they ask, answer fully.

Professor Zelle — deploy once, choose your moment

Zelle is your one roving encounter. 20g, DC 12 group roll, +1 to any attribute of their choice on a win. Deploy once, mid-Phase 1, when a crew is mid-route and has 20g+ left. Don't drop her on a table that's budget-strapped in the final minutes. Let them feel like they found her.

Timing guidance: Both Anyx and Zelle are most impactful when the crew has room to respond — not in the last five minutes when they've already committed. Work backwards from the Phase 1 cutoff and identify your window early.

Phase 2 — Sponsors & Course Reveal
Every Sponsor Tier Has Genuine Value

15–19 Elite ±3 / 2 uses Two powerful uses, each a major swing. Start the conversation about saving them for Hard tiles — not burning them on tiles they'd likely clear anyway.

Nat 1 GM Sponsor ±1 / 2 uses The pitch stumbled. You showed up anyway. Don't apologise — own it: "I'm still here. Two uses. Let's make them count."
Course Reveal — One Observation Per Build

When the map goes up, give your table thirty seconds to absorb it. Then offer one targeted observation:

V "Your first two tiles are home ground. The canyon is your test — five tiles, mostly Handling. Think about when you want sponsor support in there."
H "Segment 2 is yours. Watch the Guild Quarter Sprint in the middle — Velocity tile, Hard DC. Plan around it."
R "The back half of the race favours you. The Aether Bridge near the end is the hardest tile on the course — that's your tile. Save something for it."
BAL "No segment carries you, but none punishes you badly either. Your sponsor timing matters more than anyone else's — you don't have a safety net, but you don't have a weak spot either."

One observation. Then let them think. Not a Phase 3 strategy lecture.

Phase 3 — Race Runtime
Sponsor Timing — Core Principle

Sponsor uses are most valuable on your table's hardest tiles, not their easiest. The instinct is to save them for "something important" — which means they often don't fire at maximum value.

Elite ±3: On a Hard off-attribute tile, +3 turns ~30% into ~45–50%. Best saved for the tile type they invested least in, at the hardest difficulty on that type.
Standard ±2: The negative use is often forgotten. A −2 on the race leader at a Hard tile is frequently a better play than +2 on your own tile you'd probably clear. Fire the negative in Segment 2 when the field is bunched.
GM Sponsor ±1: Three modest uses across genuinely difficult moments. One per segment is a reasonable rhythm — three small boosts across three hard tiles beats one big moment that never comes.
