PHASE 1 — CARNIVAL MINI-ENCOUNTERS
GM Reference  ·  Three Optional Encounters  ·  No Mechanical Reward  ·  RP & Atmosphere Only
ENCOUNTER A — THE IRON HANDSHAKE
Arm Wrestling  ·  STR  ·  ~3 minutes
Orvyn Theck has dragged a plank table to one of the district thoroughfares. Tin cup. Handwritten sign: BEAT THECK — WIN RESPECT. A small crowd has formed. It always does with Theck. He finds this appropriate.
Sound: crowd murmur, forearms on wood, Theck's short laugh  ·  Smell: sawdust and spilled ale
Mechanic — d20 + STR vs. DC 14
ResultCall
Beat 14Theck nods slowly, shakes the hand, releases. Crowd reacts.
Tie (14)"Rematch. No charge." One more roll, same DC. Draw on second tie — full honours.
Miss 14Pins, releases immediately, pats the hand. No mockery.
GM Notes
Play Theck as genuinely respectful — he likes competition, never trash-talks. If a player has −3 or worse STR and insists, Theck says "Alright. I'll use my left" and adjusts to DC 10. He means it.

Nat 20: crowd reacts, Theck stands briefly — "There it is."   Nat 1: quiet "Good try" and nothing else.

If multiple players compete, run them one at a time and let the table commentate. The crowd is half the entertainment.
ENCOUNTER B — FIND THE GEAR
Shell Game  ·  WIS  ·  ~3 minutes
Three dented brass cups on an upturned crate. Under one: a small iron cog, tarnished green. Operator: Sable — no last name — moving cups with the rhythm of someone who's done this since she was nine. She narrates in a low, pleasant voice that never quite matches her hands.
Sound: soft scrape of brass on wood, Sable's quiet patter  ·  Sight: cog glimpsed one second, three moves, then stillness
Mechanic — d20 + WIS vs. DC 13
ResultCall
Beat 13Right cup. Sable lifts it without drama — "Sharp eyes."
Miss 13Wrong cup, then correct one revealed. "So close." She means it.
Nat 20They spotted the tell. Sable tilts her head. "Hm." Doesn't say what it was.
Nat 1Picked confidently and wrong. Sable glances at the cup with an expression suggesting she's thinking about something else.
GM Notes
WIS framing: don't get fooled — the winner isn't faster, they're harder to misdirect. Reward players who describe watching her hands: "Most people watch my face. You're smarter than that."

Sable doesn't cheat. Fast and misdirecting, but if someone beats her, she lost fair. She finds good observers interesting. Multiple players: she re-shuffles every attempt.

If someone wins, she hands them the cog. "Souvenir." It's worthless. It's a perfect trophy.
ENCOUNTER C — THE LONG POUR
Drinking Contest  ·  CON  ·  Last Player Standing  ·  ~5 minutes
A longer table near the festival edge. A barrel of Cogsworth Blackpipe — smells like burnt caramel and gear oil. The man running it is enormous, cheerful, goes by "Barley." Same careful pour every time. Peaceful expression of someone who has watched a great many people make this mistake before.
Sound: table getting louder each round, then quieter, then one voice  ·  Smell: Blackpipe — sweet and wrong in equal measure
Mechanic — d20 + CON each round · Out on 8 or below
Structure
Each roundAll remaining players roll d20 + CON. Anyone rolling 8 or below is out. Barley removes their cup without ceremony.
No eliminationsRound repeats — the Blackpipe keeps coming.
Final twoHead to head — single roll each, lowest is out, ties re-roll.
GM Notes
Real in-world countdown — "Round one. Bottoms up." Let the table narrate reactions between rolls. High-CON smug players will get heat from others — let that happen.

Nat 1: not just out — a moment: a slow slide sideways, a confident declaration about aerodynamics, a firm friendship with a lamppost. Light and fast.

Nat 20: Barley — "You've done this before." Not sure if it's a compliment. Winner gets his acknowledgement and a refilled cup.

Small table (2–3 players): reduce threshold to 6 or below so it doesn't end in one round.
