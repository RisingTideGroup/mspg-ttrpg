# Act 1 — The Feathermarket
### DM Reference Sheet · 10–15 Encounters

**Budget:** 100 gp per team  
**Item cap:** Max 3 items acquired (purchased or earned)  
**Time pressure:** 60 min real-world. DM keeps things moving.

---

## PURCHASABLE ITEMS (shops)
*These can be bought outright. Prices are fixed. No haggling unless the Bard or Rogue makes a case for it — DC 14 Persuasion or Deception, success = 10 gp off.*

---

### 1. GOGSWORTH'S BAKERY
**NPC:** Gogsworth — a enormous, flour-dusted goose who never stops moving and speaks entirely in baking metaphors.  
**Pitch:** *"You look underprepared. A champion needs fuel! My Thundercrust Pie has won three county fairs and one small war."*

**Item: Thundercrust Pie** · 25 gp  
*Race effect: Once per race, before any collective roll, the team may eat the pie. Add +3 to the result. Single use.*  
*Flavor: It tastes like lightning and regret. Worth it.*

**Optional interaction:** If a player asks what's in it, Gogsworth launches into a 90-second monologue. Any player who succeeds a DC 12 Insight check realizes he's describing nitrous oxide. They get a free slice as a sample — no race effect, but great RP.

---

### 2. MADAME VOSS'S SCROLLTORIUM
**NPC:** Madame Voss — an elderly goose in enormous spectacles who communicates mostly through tutting and pointing.  
**Pitch:** She doesn't pitch. She slides a scroll across the counter and stares at you.

**Item: Scroll of Track Negation** · 40 gp  
*Race effect: Destroys one hazard entirely before the roll is made. The hazard is narrated as having "simply not happened." One use.*  
*Flavor: The scroll smells faintly of burning rubber. Madame Voss says nothing about this.*

**Optional interaction:** The Wizard can identify it with Detect Magic as Abjuration school, confirming it's real. The Druid's Find Traps cantrip causes it to glow slightly — it's technically a trap suppressor.

---

### 3. IRONBEAK FORGE
**NPC:** Ironbeak — a one-winged goose blacksmith who lost the wing "in a race, don't ask." He speaks in clipped sentences and always has a hammer in hand.  
**Pitch:** *"Aero fin. Forged it myself. Goes on the front. Cuts corners cleaner."*

**Item: Ironbeak Aero Fin** · 35 gp  
*Race effect: +2 to all Cornering hazard rolls for the entire race.*  
*Flavor: It's heavy but perfectly balanced. Ironbeak nods once when you take it. That's the most emotion he's shown in years.*

**Optional interaction:** The Fighter or Barbarian can ask about his missing wing. DC 13 Persuasion gets the story — he lost it on a chicane, car flipped, he walked away. He respects racers. He'll throw in a set of **Ironbeak Brake Weights** (minor item, see below) for free if a player rolls 15+ Persuasion.

**Bonus minor item: Ironbeak Brake Weights** (free with persuasion or 15 gp separately)  
*Race effect: Once, when a Cornering roll fails, reduce the penalty by half.*

---

### 4. THE FIZZING FLAGON (tavern)
**NPC:** Brasso — a young goose barkeep who is VERY excited about the race and has absolutely no idea what he's talking about.  
**Pitch:** *"I made a drink for the occasion! It's called the Straight Line. It's got speed in it. Probably."*

**Item: The Straight Line (bottled)** · 20 gp  
*Race effect: +3 to one Straight-Line Speed hazard roll. Single use.*  
*Flavor: It's bright orange, smells like petrol, and makes your feathers stand up. Brasso is very proud.*

**Optional interaction:** The Bard can perform here for 5 minutes (DC 13 Performance). Success: Brasso gives them a second bottle for free. The whole table cheers. The Announcer can be notified — good story moment.

---

### 5. RELICS & REMNANTS (antiquities shop)
**NPC:** Thessaly — a slow-speaking, ancient goose who may or may not be partially asleep at all times.  
**Pitch:** *"...found it in the old track ruins... don't know what it does... probably fine..."*

**Item: Rune of Velocity (mystery item)** · 30 gp  
*The team doesn't know the effect until they use it. DM rolls secretly:*  
- *1–2: +4 to any one roll (excellent)*
- *3–4: +2 to any one roll (good)*
- *5: Reroll a failed pit stop check (solid)*
- *6: No effect, but the car looks impressive (useless, great story)*

*Flavor: It pulses faintly. Thessaly adds "probably fine" one more time as you leave.*

**Optional interaction:** The Wizard's Detect Magic or Arcana (DC 14) can identify it as Transmutation, narrowing the table to 1–4. The Rogue's Investigation (DC 15) finds a maker's mark — it's from a champion who won this circuit forty years ago. DM may award a free inspiration die.

## EARNED ITEMS (encounters)
*These cannot be purchased. They require a skill challenge, roleplay, or combat encounter to obtain.*

---

### 6. THE STREET GANG (The Skid Marks)
**Setup:** A rough-looking gang of geese in matching leather jackets blocks a side alley. They've got a glowing rune painted on the wall — someone's personal racing charm.  
**Trigger:** Party tries to pass through or the Rogue spots the rune (DC 13 Perception).

**Three paths to the item:**
- **Fight:** CR adjusted — 3 gang members, each with 12 HP, +3 to hit, 1d6 damage. Defeating them earns the rune. (This is the 1-2 combat encounter for Act 1.)
- **Intimidate:** DC 15 Intimidation (Barbarian Rage active = advantage). They back down and leave the rune as tribute.
- **Deceive:** DC 14 Deception — convince them you're the race officials and the rune is being "confiscated for inspection."

**Item: Skid Mark Rune** (earned only)  
*Race effect: Once per race, when the team fails a roll, they may treat it as one bracket higher (e.g., slow → medium). The rune cracks and is spent.*  
*Flavor: It smells like burnt rubber and ego.*

---

### 7. THE TINKER'S CART (roadside encounter)
**NPC:** Pip — a frantic young goose inventor whose cart has just spectacularly broken down. Gears everywhere. She's sitting in the middle of it all crying slightly.  
**Setup:** Party walks past. Pip calls out for help.

**Challenge:** Help Pip fix her cart.  
- Athletics DC 12 to lift the axle  
- Tinker's Tools / Artificer knowledge / Druid's Nature (DC 13) to identify the fault  
- Any healing spell or the Cleric's Mending cantrip fixes a cracked component instantly (auto-success)

**Item: Pip's Prototype Stabilizer** (earned — no gold cost)  
*Race effect: Negates the penalty from one failed Pit Stop roll completely. One use. Pip insists it "should" work.*  
*Flavor: It's held together with twine and optimism. Pip waves you off with tears in her eyes and whispers "win one for science."*

---

### 8. THE ORACLE OF THE INNER RING
**NPC:** A hooded goose sitting alone on an overturned crate, with a small crystal ball that shows... a racetrack?  
**Setup:** The Oracle will tell one team member one true thing about the race. No cost. But they must answer a riddle first.

**The Riddle:** *"I am fastest when I am still. I am strongest when I yield. I am loudest in the moment of silence. What am I?"*  
Answer: *The moment before the start gun fires.* (Accept any creative answer that involves anticipation, preparation, or the starting line. Don't be rigid.)

**Item: Oracle's Token** (earned — solve the riddle)  
*Race effect: Once per race, before any roll, the Race Engineer may declare "we saw this coming." The team rolls with advantage.*  
*Flavor: The crystal ball shows your car, briefly, ahead of everyone else. Then it goes dark.*

**DM note:** If the Druid or Cleric tries Insight (DC 12), they sense the Oracle is entirely genuine and slightly sad. She wanted to race once. Good small story.

---

### 9. THE BETTING STALL (risk encounter)
**NPC:** Flink — a fast-talking goose bookie with a clipboard and an expression that has never once been surprised.  
**Pitch:** *"Double or nothing, friends. I've got a prop bet for you. You win, you walk away with something special. You lose... well. You lose 20 gp."*

**The Bet (DM's choice of one):**
- Arm wrestle Flink's bouncer (Athletics DC 14)
- Name three famous race circuits (History DC 12 — or the DM accepts any three real F1 circuits)
- Do something that makes the whole table laugh (Performance, no DC — DM judgment)

**Item: Flink's Lucky Slipstream Token** (earned via bet — costs 20 gp to enter, win to get item, lose 20 gp with nothing)  
*Race effect: Once per race, after another team's roll is announced, your team may "draft off them" — add half their roll result (rounded down) as a bonus to your own next roll.*  
*Flavor: It's a coin with both sides the same. Flink says this is normal.*

---

### 10. THE TEMPLE OF THE STARTING FLAG
**NPC:** Deacon Yort — a solemn, slow-moving goose in racing vestments who takes this very seriously.  
**Setup:** A small roadside shrine to the spirit of the race. Incense burning. A donation box.

**Three ways to earn the blessing:**
- **Donate** 15 gp to the shrine
- **Pray sincerely** (Roleplay — DM awards this freely for genuine character-appropriate prayer)
- **Paladin or Cleric** performing any religious rite auto-succeeds

**Item: Blessing of the Starting Flag**  
*Race effect: Once, when the entire team rolls (not just some), and ALL dice show 10 or higher, the blessing activates automatically — the team gains +4 to that roll as a divine wind pushes from behind. Passive trigger, no action required.*  
*Flavor: Deacon Yort nods gravely. "The Flag sees you."*

---

### 11. THE RIVAL TEAM'S MECHANIC (social intrigue)
**NPC:** Grix — clearly wearing another team's colors. Nervous. Eating a sandwich alone.  
**Setup:** The Rogue spots (DC 12 Perception) that Grix has a component that looks suspiciously useful. He's having second thoughts about his team.

**Paths:**
- **Persuade him to defect:** DC 15 Persuasion — he gives them the part and wishes them luck
- **Bribe him:** 25 gp — he looks both ways and slides it over
- **Rogue Sleight of Hand:** DC 14 — lifted cleanly. If failed, Grix notices but doesn't chase (he's not getting involved)

**Item: Rival's Tuned Exhaust Manifold** (social/stolen)  
*Race effect: +2 to all Straight-Line Speed hazard rolls for the entire race.*  
*Flavor: It's engraved with another team's name. The DM can narrate that team struggling on the straight in Act 3.*

---

### 12. THE WANDERING KNIGHT (random encounter)
**NPC:** Sir Haulwick — an ancient, fully armored goose on a horse who is deeply confused about what a race is but extremely willing to help.  
**Setup:** He approaches the party and announces he wishes to bestow a boon upon worthy champions.

**Challenge:** Impress him with a display of valor or virtue.  
- Fighter or Paladin describing their combat history (auto-success)
- Any character performing an act of genuine selflessness during Act 1 (DM awards)
- DC 13 Persuasion describing the race as a noble tournament

**Item: Sir Haulwick's Favor (pennant)** (earned)  
*Race effect: Once per race, when the team is in last place on the leaderboard entering a lap, they gain +3 to all rolls that lap (the underdog surge). Does not apply if not in last place.*  
*Flavor: It's a tiny pennant he pulls from inside his armor. It smells like the 12th century.*

---

### 13. THE INFORMATION BROKER (bonus encounter)
**NPC:** Whisper — a hooded figure at a corner table in the Fizzing Flagon. Offers "track intelligence."  
**Pitch:** *"I know what's on the track. Specific hazards. Not for free."*

**Cost:** 30 gp OR a successful DC 15 Intimidation/Deception  
**Item:** Not a physical item — instead, the team learns ONE specific hazard from the race in advance (DM's choice, ideally a Lap 2 or 3 hazard). They can plan for it.

*Race effect: Team rolls with advantage on that one specific pre-revealed hazard. One use.*  
*Flavor: Whisper leaves immediately after. The information is correct.*

---

### 14. THE CRASH SITE (exploration encounter)
**Setup:** A smoking, wrecked vehicle sits in an alley off the main market. No driver in sight. Scattered parts glow faintly.

**Paths:**
- **Investigate the wreck:** Investigation DC 13 — find a salvageable part in the debris
- **Druid uses Find Traps:** Reveals the debris is trapped — a spring-loaded panel. Wisdom DC 12 to disarm safely (Druid advantage). Disarming earns the item without risk.
- **Just grab it:** Dex DC 11 to avoid the trap. Fail = 1d6 piercing damage, still get the item.

**Item: Salvaged Boost Coil** (exploration)  
*Race effect: Once per race, the team may declare "we're burning the coil" before a roll. Add +4. After use, the DM notes the car takes cosmetic damage — purely narrative, no penalty.*  
*Flavor: It hums. Nobody knows what it was originally part of. Nobody asks.*

---

### 15. THE TRACK INSPECTOR (wild card encounter)
**NPC:** Overseer Prenn — an officious, clipboard-carrying goose who is absolutely certain your car is in violation of something.  
**Setup:** He stops your team to "inspect the vehicle" (the car doesn't technically exist yet, which he is ignoring).

**Challenge:** Get through the inspection without losing time or gold.
- **Persuade:** DC 14 — "everything is in order, sir"
- **Bard uses Performance:** DC 12 — distract him with a song long enough that he forgets why he stopped you
- **Paladin's Zone of Truth countermeasure:** The Cleric or Paladin's Zone of Truth actually backfires here — Prenn knows the car isn't real and is MORE suspicious. The Wizard's Suggestion (DC 12 Wis save from Prenn) works perfectly.
- **Failure:** Lose 10 minutes of Act 1 time (DM narrates a delay — no gold penalty, just a time flavor hit)

**Reward for clever resolution:** Prenn accidentally leaves behind his **Official Inspection Stamp**.

**Item: Official Inspection Stamp**  
*Race effect: Once, when the team fails a Pit Stop roll, show the stamp — the failure is reclassified as a "regulatory hold" instead of a penalty. No effect on the roll, but the leaderboard delay is halved.*  
*Flavor: It shouldn't work. It does.*
