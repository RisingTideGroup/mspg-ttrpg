SETUP — READ TO PLAYERS
Zelle doesn't look up from her field journal when you arrive. A cup of tea sits beside her — cold, forgotten. She's crouched in a doorway, and the instrument in her other hand is measuring something you'd need more context to identify.

When she does look up, she looks at your construct first. A long look — the kind that is clearly calculating something.

"Interesting configuration. Slightly unusual weight distribution in the rear third."

She's right. You don't know how she knew that.

"I was working on something." She turns the journal to show you a page of four annotated schematics — four competing designs, each with a different fuel system, stabiliser type, and the name of the theoretical engineer who proposed it. "I'm trying to sort out which design belongs to whom. The notes are intact, but the attribution got scrambled."

She pulls a folded card from her coat pocket and holds it out.

"Four engineers. Four designs. All the information you need is here. Give me the correct attribution, and we'll have a conversation about your construct."
Mechanic Summary
Players work together verbally, then call their answer as a group. On a correct answer: roll DC 10 (group average). Pass → 20g for +1 to any attribute. Fail → no purchase. Wrong answer → no roll, no purchase. See page 2 for full GM delivery notes.
THE PUZZLE CARD — READ ALOUD
Four constructs entered last season's Cogsworth Calibration Trials by four different engineers: Ashwick, Brenn, Voss, and Dray.
Each ran a different fuel system (coal-gas, aether cell, pressure steam, arcane compound), used a different stabiliser (gyroscopic, torsion coil, magnetic damper, pendulum brace), and placed in a different finishing position (1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th).
  1. 1Ashwick's construct did not use coal-gas or arcane compound.
  2. 2The gyroscopic stabiliser finished in 1st place.
  3. 3Brenn's construct used pressure steam.
  4. 4The construct with the magnetic damper finished directly ahead of Ashwick's construct — one place higher.
  5. 5Voss did not finish in 4th place.
  6. 6The arcane compound construct used a pendulum brace.
  7. 7Dray's construct finished in 3rd place.
The Question — all players call simultaneously
"Which stabiliser did Voss's construct use?"
Hint — Only If Stuck After 3–4 Minutes
Zelle looks up, without emphasis: "The finishing positions tend to unlock the rest." That is the only hint she offers. She delivers it the way she'd deliver any observation — flatly, without drama.

GM DELIVERY NOTES
Before the Session
Write the 7 clues on an index card for yourself. Keep this solution sheet handy. Do not prepare a handout — players work from what they hear and remember. That's intentional: the group has to hold the logic together verbally, which is where the collaboration lives.
Running the Encounter
Read clues aloud one at a time, at a pace the group can follow. Pause between each. Let them ask you to re-read individual clues — that's fine, Zelle doesn't mind repeating herself. What she won't do is interpret or rephrase.

Zelle is already writing in her journal while they think. She is not impatient. She is occupied.
Tone
Zelle is not a teacher giving a test. She's an academic who found a puzzle and is delighted when someone else engages with it genuinely. Play her as curious, not evaluating. The answer matters to her because the problem matters to her — not because she's gatekeeping.

She will not be visibly excited when they get it right. She will write it down and close the journal. That's the tell.
Deduction Path — If You Need to Follow Along

Clue 7: Dray → 3rd. Clue 4: magnetic damper is one place ahead of Ashwick; if Ashwick is 4th, magnetic damper is 3rd → Dray → magnetic damper, Ashwick → 4th. Clue 2: gyroscopic → 1st. Voss and Brenn take 1st and 2nd. Remaining fuel for Voss and Dray: coal-gas and arcane compound. Dray has magnetic damper, not pendulum brace → Dray ≠ arcane compound → Voss → arcane compound → pendulum brace. Dray → coal-gas. Brenn → 1st → gyroscopic. Ashwick → torsion coil. Voss → 2nd.
