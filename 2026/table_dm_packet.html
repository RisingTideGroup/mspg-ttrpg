What Is This Game

The Golden Goots Grand Prix is a tabletop RPG one-shot built for a room full of people. Think Soapbox Derby meets Mario Kart meets Formula One — in a steampunk city called Cogsworth, where racing constructs are built, bought, and battered across a three-segment street circuit.

Every table plays simultaneously. Your table is one crew. You run Phase 1 and Phase 2 independently. In Phase 3 the Race DM runs the circuit for the whole room and narrates all outcomes. Your job shifts to point person for rolls and voice for your table.

Constructs can only equip three upgrades. Crews can earn more than three parts across Phase 1 — but they must choose which three go on the construct before Phase 2 begins. Choose wisely.

NinjaOne is sponsoring real rewards for the top three finishing tables. The stakes are genuine.

The One Mechanic You Need to Know

Group Challenge Roll (Phase 1): All players roll d20 at the same time. Sum all results, divide by number of players, round down. That average is compared to the DC. Beat it = success. Miss it = lower tier or no unlock. Announce every Nat 20 before you calculate — build the drama first.

To get parts, players can: Pay gold to Buy any available tier · Win a Challenge Roll to unlock better tiers · Win a Battle at a contested vendor stall.

01 Grimshaw's Combustion Works NPC: Grimshaw The Main Floor
Atmosphere You smell it before you see it — hot metal and machine oil, thick in the air. The foundry floor is massive and has never once been quiet. Steam drifts between machines that have been running longer than most constructs on the circuit have existed. Grimshaw has been working this floor for thirty years. The Grand Prix came through town and changed who buys from him. He hasn't decided if that's a good thing yet. He doesn't look up when you enter. He is waiting to see if you are worth looking up for.
Grimshaw Characteristics: Large, slow-moving, unhurried. A voice like an engine idling. He doesn't raise it. He stands with his arms loose at his sides and watches until he decides what he thinks of you. Then he'll tell you.
He can answer:
— "What makes the Signature Core worth 38 gold?" "It doesn't fail at the wrong moment. The standard one does. Whether that matters depends on how fast you're going when it happens."
— "What do you think of our build?" He looks at the crew's list for a long time. Then: "You're buying speed and hoping the road is kind. It won't be."

Phase 1 — Random Encounters

Three encounters are scripted and ready to run. You can drop one whenever you feel like it — between vendor visits, when pacing needs a jolt, or when players look like they want a fight. They are optional. Keep them short: each should resolve in five to ten minutes.

When to trigger: We recommend rolling a d6 or d12 after every other vendor visit. On a 1, the crew triggers a random encounter before reaching the next shop. Roll a d3 (or choose) to determine which encounter fires. You can also skip the roll entirely and just drop one when the table energy calls for it — you know your table.

You can run each encounter more than once if it fits. The Desperate Crew in particular can reappear — different crew, same problem.

ENCOUNTER 01 The Desperate Crew TRIGGER: Between any two vendor visits · Works anywhere in the district map
The Setup Three rival racers cut across the crew's path — work gear, harried expressions, and the unmistakable look of people who have spent all their gold on the wrong things. They've run out and they know it. One of them is holding a wrench in a way that isn't quite threatening but isn't quite not.

"Look. We just need forty gold. Or — and we're not saying this is what's happening — you could just hand over whatever you bought at Grimshaw's."

They're not skilled fighters. They're desperate ones, which is sometimes worse.

ENCOUNTER 02 The Guild Enforcer TRIGGER: After visiting the Bazaar · Best placed near the Guild Hall
The Setup A Guild rep — not the one behind the desk, a different one, bigger, with a sealed envelope — steps out of a side entrance and falls into step beside the crew.

"Congratulations on your registration. We've noticed you've been shopping without a Bazaar Trading Licence. That's a 25g fee, same-day processing, very reasonable."

There is no such licence. This is extortion, conducted with such bureaucratic confidence that it takes a moment to identify as such.

ENCOUNTER 03 The Blowback TRIGGER: After anyone buys Corra's overclock cells · OR any time near the Hot Works
The Setup A racer from a different crew comes around a corner at speed, one arm smoking faintly, expression on their face that is specifically the expression of someone whose construct has just detonated a fuel cell in the Forge Quarter staging area.

They are not looking where they're going. They are looking at whoever sold them those cells. And they have just located Corra's stall — which is right behind the crew.

"OUT OF THE WAY. I NEED A WORD WITH SOMEONE."

The crew is between them and Corra. This is now the crew's problem.

Phase 2 — The Sponsor Pavilion

Phase 2 has four steps. You run all of them at your table before the room comes together for the roll.

Step 1 — Choose Your Three Upgrades

The construct can only equip three upgrades, regardless of how many parts the crew bought. Before anything else in Phase 2, the table must agree on which three parts go on the construct. Parts not equipped are left behind. This decision shapes the whole race — choose based on the segments that matter most to your build.

Give the table a few minutes. Facilitate the conversation if needed. When they've decided, have them write the three chosen upgrades on their character sheets or on the dry-erase board.

Step 2 — Name the Crew & Draw the Construct

Each table has a dry-erase board. The crew picks a team name and draws their construct on the board. It doesn't need to be good art — it needs to feel like theirs. The board travels with them through Phase 3. Encourage them to label it with the team name and their three equipped upgrades.

Step 3 — Determine Racing Strengths & Present to the Room

Based on their three equipped upgrades, the crew determines their racing strengths — which segments they're built for and what their construct does best. They present this to the room together as a team. Not one rep: the whole table makes the case for their construct.

This is the pitch. Encourage theatrics. A crew that commits to the bit makes the whole room better.

Step 4 — Roll the Large d20 in Front of the Room

After the pitch, the crew rolls the large d20 in front of the whole room. This is a public moment. The Race DM announces the sponsor tier. Every table watches every other table roll.

Phase 3 — The Race · Your Role

The Race DM runs the circuit for the whole room. The Race DM explains all roll outcomes — good and bad. Your players roll d20 at your table, you call the number to the Race DM, and they tell the room what it means. You do not judge success or failure.

Your job in Phase 3 is to manage your table's rolls and be the crew's voice. Use the tile summaries below to brief your table before each roll and guide strategy discussion. The Race DM narrates everything that happens on the circuit. You can flag the Race DM with a question at any point — they are your resource throughout.
