Phase 2 Overview
When Immediately after Phase 1 closes. GM at each table signals MC when their table is done buying.
Duration ~20 min for up to 10 tables · ~90 sec per table for presentation + sponsor roll.
What happens Each table presents crew and construct. One rep rolls for their sponsor. MC reveals the patron. Course map displayed. Race rules briefed. Race begins.
MC materials Sponsor patron pool (tier-sorted). Course map on projector. This guide. Room tracker ready.
Sequence Presentations → Sponsor rolls → Initiative roll → Break → Course reveal → Phase 3.
Opening — MC Cold Open
Phase 1 is over. The gold is spent. The parts are bolted. Your constructs are as ready as they are going to get. Now it’s time to step out of the workshop and into the Sponsor Pavilion — because every crew racing tonight needs a backer. We’re going to hear from each table, one at a time. You’ll introduce your crew and your construct to the sponsors in this room. Then you’ll roll to see what kind of support you’ve earned. Tables — get your crew name and construct ready. We’ll start at Table 1.
⚙  If tables aren’t all done, it’s fine to start Phase 2 while the last 1–2 tables finish their final purchase. Don’t let the room go cold waiting.
Per-Table Sequence — Repeat for Every Table
~90 sec per table. Stay tight — energy drops if presentations drag.
1
Call the Table
Table [NUMBER] — who speaks for your crew? Stand up, tell us your crew name, your construct, and make your pitch to the sponsors.
Rep delivers pitch. 30–60 sec ideal. “Love it — now let’s see what the sponsors think” if a table runs long.
2
The Persuasion Roll
Thank you. Now — roll your persuasion. One person from your crew picks up one d20. Let’s see what kind of sponsor you can attract.
⚙  Nat 20: announce immediately — “Table [X] just rolled a Natural 20.” Pause. Room reacts. Nat 1: equally public. Their GM steps up as backer. Play warmly.

4
Reveal the Patron
Draw a community legend patron from the matching tier pool. Deliver as a character moment — the patron has a presence and a reason they back this crew.

5
Log and Move On
Table [NUMBER] — [Patron] is in your corner. [Tier], [uses] uses, [modifier]. Mark it on your card. Table [NEXT] — you’re up. Who speaks for your crew?
⚙  Don’t let the room idle. Call the next table before the last one finishes writing.
Initiative Roll
Before we race — we need to know who goes first in Round 1. Every player picks up a d20. On my count — three, two, one, roll. GMs — add up your results and divide by the number of players. Round down. Call out your number.
1
All tables roll simultaneously on MC’s count.
2
Each GM sums results ÷ player count (round down). Call score to MC.
3
Ties: re-roll between tied tables only — single die, highest wins.
4
MC announces Round 1 order. From Round 2: reverse grid — last place first.

Key MC Reminders
Natural 20: Stop everything. “Table [X] just rolled a Natural 20.” Pause. Let the room react. Then reveal the patron. Elite Enhanced — three uses of ±3.
Natural 1: Their GM is their backer. Play it warmly — never as a punishment. Two uses of ±1 is still two uses.
Transitions: Call the next table before the last one finishes writing. The room’s energy drops with every second of dead air between presentations.
